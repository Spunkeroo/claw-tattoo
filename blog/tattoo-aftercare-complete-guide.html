Complete Tattoo Aftercare Guide: Healing Tips That Actually Work

By claw.tattoo | March 1, 2026 | 8 min read

Day-by-Day Healing Timeline

Days 1-3: Your tattoo is an open wound. Keep the initial wrap on for 2-4 hours (or as your artist instructs). Gently wash with lukewarm water and unscented soap. Pat dry with a clean paper towel. Apply a thin layer of unscented moisturizer. Days 4-14: Peeling and flaking begins — this is normal. DO NOT pick or scratch. Continue washing and moisturizing 2-3 times daily. Days 15-30: Surface healing completes but deeper layers are still healing. The tattoo may look cloudy — this is normal and will clear up. Months 1-3: Full healing. Keep moisturized and protect from sun.

Best Aftercare Products

Aquaphor Healing Ointment — the most recommended product by tattoo artists for the first 3-5 days. Apply a THIN layer — too much suffocates the skin. Lubriderm Daily Moisture (unscented) — excellent for the peeling/flaking phase. Hustle Butter Deluxe — all-natural, vegan tattoo aftercare butter. Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser — perfect for washing fresh tattoos. SPF 50+ sunscreen — essential for healed tattoos exposed to sun.

What NOT to Do

1. Don't submerge in water for 2-3 weeks — no pools, baths, hot tubs, or ocean. Showers are fine. 2. Don't pick or scratch peeling skin — you'll pull out ink and cause patchy healing. 3. Don't use Neosporin — it can cause allergic reactions and draw ink out. 4. Don't expose to direct sunlight — UV destroys fresh tattoo pigment. 5. Don't wear tight clothing over the tattoo — friction causes irritation. 6. Don't exercise intensely for 48-72 hours — sweat irritates fresh tattoos.

When to See a Doctor

Some redness, swelling, and warmth are normal for the first few days. See a doctor if: 1) Redness spreads significantly beyond the tattoo after day 3. 2) You develop a fever over 101°F. 3) Pus (yellow/green discharge) appears — clear fluid is normal, colored fluid is not. 4) The tattoo feels hot to the touch after the first week. 5) Red streaks extend from the tattoo toward your heart (sign of blood infection). Infections are rare with proper aftercare but should be treated immediately.

Long-Term Tattoo Care

A healed tattoo still needs care. Always apply SPF 50+ when exposing tattoos to sunlight — UV is the #1 cause of fading. Stay hydrated — well-hydrated skin keeps tattoos vibrant. Moisturize regularly — dry skin dulls tattoo colors. Touch-ups are normal, especially for fine line work and watercolor styles. Most artists offer free touch-ups within the first year. Consider a touch-up every 5-10 years for optimal vibrancy.
